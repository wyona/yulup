2. Overview

Entangled XML (eXML) is a well-formed and valid XML document, containing a part of non-well-formed interleaved XML. The eXML format thereby acts as a container, providing all the necessary information to derive every document making up the interleaved document. This means that the eXML, among other things, declares the number of individual documents contained in the entangled section, as well as provides the namespace declarations used by these documents.

Note that the individual documents making up the non-well-formed entangled content may by themselves not be valid or even well-formed. Nevertheless, the eXML gives enough information to derive every document from the entangled form, and reconstruct its original content as it was before the interleaving.
